LoRaWAN KiCad I2C Sensors IoT

Project Overview

Designed for smart digital agriculture, this modular device collects critical metrics where cellular and Wi-Fi networks fail. It transmits encoded data over miles (tested up to 12 km in zero visibility) using The Things Network's LoRa sensor infrastructure.

Hardware Architecture

The development went from a breadboard phase to a 4-layer PCB designed in KiCad, strictly adhering to trace antenna constraints and optimized isolation zones.

  • Efficient Brain: Atmel SAMD21 microcontroller, perfect for its low-frequency clock that runs in the deepest 'Sleep' mode, waking up via internal hardware timers.
  • RF Transceiver: HopeRF RFM95 module operating at 868MHz, requiring exact capacitor calibration to boost the Tx signal power strictly within the levels permitted by the European Union.
  • Thermal and Power Management: Equipped with a Texas Instruments BQ24072C IC, it harvests tiny energy pulses from a 5V/200mA mini solar panel to keep a 1500mAh LFP cell perpetually charged.

Low-Power Engineering

By disabling unused pull-up pins, using P-Channel MOSFETs to cut power to sensors (when unneeded), and employing an RTC (Real Time Clock), the idle, silent circuit draws a mere ~3.5 microAmperes of current.
