Analog Audio VCF Design PCB Routing

Philosophy and Vision

This Voltage Controlled Filter module stems from a deep love for old-school audio design, focusing on a dirty yet rich sound full of the pleasant harmonics typical of operational amplifier saturation. It drops into a standard 3U Eurorack case and requires the typical ±12V power supply standard.

Circuit Features

The design is based on the venerable LM13700 OTA chip in a 4th-order low-pass configuration, delivering a steep frequency response of 24 decibels per octave.

  • Input Mixer: Three passively mixed audio inputs coupled into an LM324 buffer, allowing users to feed multiple complex waveforms and generate dense sounds prior to filtering.
  • Feedback Resonance: A feedback path from the final stage back to the first, with exponential control on the VCA pole, produces an extremely aggressive and responsive resonance squeal (capable of self-oscillation without any stimulus signal at the input).
  • Manual and PCB Techniques: The internal buffers of the LM13700s suffer from thermal distortion; thus, extra low-noise transistors (BC547/557) were used as substitute Darlington pairs for a much more transparent sonic result up until the desired saturation kicks in.

The Visual Panel

To interact immediately in a dim studio environment (or live setting), the entire metallic potentiometer interface features golden indicator panels (ENIG PCB fabrication) over the board's silky black soldermask, marrying tactile performance with geometric precision.
